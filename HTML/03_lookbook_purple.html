From the Creative Director

A Letter to You

Dear Friends,

As I write this letter, I am filled with immense pride and gratitude. The Velvet collection represents not just the culmination of months of creative work, but a profound journey into the very essence of luxury and beauty.

This season, we drew inspiration from the opulent courts of Renaissance Europe, where artisans dedicated their lives to creating objects of extraordinary beauty. We visited museums in Florence, studied ancient textile techniques in Lyon, and spent countless hours in our atelier perfecting every detail.

The result is a collection that honors tradition while embracing the modern woman's desire for elegance that transcends trends. Each piece in the Velvet collection has been crafted to make you feel not just beautiful, but powerful and confident.

I invite you to discover the stories woven into every thread, the passion infused in every stitch, and the dreams embodied in every silhouette. This is more than fashion; this is art you wear.

With love and gratitude,

Isabelle Laurent

CREATIVE DIRECTOR

Materials

The finest textiles from around the world, selected for their exceptional quality and beauty.

Silk Velvet

Our signature silk velvet is woven in Lyon, France, using techniques dating back to the Renaissance. Each meter requires days of careful production, resulting in a fabric with unparalleled depth and luminosity.

ORIGIN: LYON, FRANCE

Mikado Silk

Sourced from Japan, this structured silk provides the perfect foundation for our architectural silhouettes. Its subtle sheen and crisp drape create clean lines that define modern elegance.

ORIGIN: KYOTO, JAPAN

Cashmere Blend

Our cashmere is sourced from Mongolia and blended with silk for added strength and sheen. This luxurious fabric lines our outerwear, providing warmth without weight.

ORIGIN: MONGOLIA

Essential Guide

Garment Care

Preserve the beauty of your Velvet pieces with proper care and attention.

1

Professional Cleaning

We recommend professional dry cleaning for all Velvet garments. Choose a specialist experienced with luxury fabrics and couture details.

2

Storage

Store garments in the provided garment bags in a cool, dry place. Use padded hangers for gowns and never fold velvet fabrics.

3

Velvet Care

For minor marks on velvet, steam gently from the reverse side. Never iron directly on velvet as this will crush the pile.

4

Embellishments

Handle beaded and embroidered pieces with care. Store flat when possible and avoid contact with rough surfaces.

5

Restoration Services

Our atelier offers restoration and alteration services for all Velvet pieces. Contact your boutique for details.
