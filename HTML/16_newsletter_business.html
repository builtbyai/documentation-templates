The AI Investment Thesis: Finding Value in a Crowded Market

By Sarah Mitchell, Chief Investment Strategist | 8 min read

The artificial intelligence boom has created a familiar pattern in financial markets: initial euphoria, rapid capital deployment, and inevitable questions about valuations. As we close 2024, discerning investors face a critical question - where does genuine opportunity lie amid the noise?

Our analysis suggests the market is in a "picks and shovels" phase, where infrastructure providers and enabling technologies may offer better risk-adjusted returns than consumer-facing applications. The companies building the foundation - cloud computing, semiconductor design, and data management - possess structural advantages that pure-play AI startups cannot easily replicate.

Market Outlook

2025: Navigating Cross Currents

The incoming year presents investors with a complex mosaic of opportunities and risks. Moderating inflation, resilient employment, and corporate earnings growth provide fundamental support, while geopolitical tensions and policy uncertainty demand tactical flexibility.

Our base case assumes a "soft landing" scenario with GDP growth moderating to 2.1% and the Federal Reserve executing two rate cuts by mid-year. However, we assign meaningful probability to both upside and downside scenarios.
